# Review App — Guide for Toshifumi (Japanese trips)

*Plain-English how-to. You don't need to know anything technical — this is only how to
review and correct the Japanese text and audio for each trip.*

## What this tool does
It replaces the old "Google Doc + audio editor" process. You open a trip, correct the
Japanese text, fix the audio, listen, mark each part done, and submit. David then approves it.

## 1. Log in
1. Open the link David sends you.
2. Enter your **username** and **password**. If it doesn't work, message David — don't share
   the link publicly.

## 2. Find your trips
- The home page shows the trips waiting for you (only Japanese trips are shown to you).
- Any trip with a **pin** at the top is urgent — do those first.
- Click a trip to open it.

## 3. Review each part, from top to bottom
A trip has, in order: the **trip Title**, the **trip Description**, then the **Scenes**. Each
scene has a **Title**, the **Narration** (called *SceneDesc*), and sometimes a **Question**
with **Options**.

Read each part against the English and **correct any mistakes**.

## 4. Important: which line is spoken
Each Japanese narration has **two lines**:
- **Kanji line** (top) — the written form.
- **Kana line** (bottom) — the reading.

**The voice speaks the KANA line.** So:
- To change **the audio**, you must edit the **kana** line.
- Editing only the kanji line changes the written text but **not** the audio. The app shows a
  small reminder under the narration to make this clear.

## 5. Fix the audio after you change the words
1. Correct the **kana** line.
2. Click **"Generate from edit"** — the app re-records **just the part you changed** and keeps
   the rest of the original recording.
3. **Listen** to the new version.
4. If it's good, click **"Combine"** to keep it.
5. If it's not right:
   - **"Regenerate All"** re-records the entire line, or
   - **"Create new"** lets you attach a fresh take **with a note** for David.

Notes:
- If you only changed the **kanji** line, there is nothing new to record — the app will remind
  you that **the voice follows the kana line**.
- Occasionally the app can't re-record just your edit cleanly; it then re-records the whole
  narration and tells you so — simply **re-listen to the full clip**.

## 6. Fix a spot by highlighting it (no text change needed)
Sometimes the words are right but the **sound** is wrong — a mispronounced word, a noise, a
pause that's too short or too long. **Highlight the spot in the KANA line**, then use the
buttons under the audio player:

- **Regenerate highlighted** — makes a fresh recording of just the highlighted phrase.
- **Fix pronunciation…** — speaks *different* kana at the highlighted spot (e.g. write a clearer
  reading). The text on screen doesn't change — only the sound.
- **Trim highlighted noise** — highlight where an unwanted noise or leftover sound is, and
  the app cleans it out.
- **Insert 1s / Insert 0.5s** — click just after a 。 and the pause there gets 1 second
  (or half a second) longer.
- **Remove 1s / Remove 0.5s** — the opposite: click at a pause that is too long and it is
  shortened by up to that amount (a natural pause is always kept — it never cuts into words).

**Trim highlighted noise** also works on **question and answer audio** — highlight the spot
in that field's own text box (the kana part).

Then **listen**, and **Combine** if it used a new recording. Highlighting in the **kanji**
line doesn't work — the app will ask you to highlight in the kana line instead. If it can't
do something safely at that exact spot it will say so — nothing is changed silently.

## 7. Mark each part "Done"
- **Play the audio all the way to the end** (the progress bar must fill). Only then does the
  **Done** button unlock. Click it.
- Text-only parts (with no audio) can be marked Done straight away.

## 8. Submit the trip
- When **every** part is marked Done, click **Submit**. David reviews and approves it.
- After you submit, the trip becomes read-only until David approves or sends it back.

## 9. The AI review — answer it, then submit again
After you submit, an automated checker reads your edits. It looks at **meaning** (does the
Japanese still say what the English says?), **wording**, and whether the **questions and answers**
still make sense. It never changes anything by itself.

If it has questions, the trip **comes straight back to you** — not to David — and you'll get an
email. At the top of the trip you'll see a purple **"AI review"** box listing everything it
flagged.

- **Click any item to jump to that scene.** The same remark appears again right next to the
  text it's about, so you can read it and the text together.
- Each one has three buttons. Pick one:
  - **"I've actioned this"** — you agree, and you've made the change.
  - **"Keep my version…"** — you disagree. **You must write a short note** saying why. David
    reads your note instead of the change, so this is how you overrule the AI.
  - **"For the admin (English)"** — the problem is with the **English** text, which isn't yours
    to fix. It goes to David. You can add a note if you want.

**Every item needs an answer.** Once they all have one, click **Submit** again and the trip goes
to David. If you try to submit with items unanswered, the app will tell you.

The AI is often right, but not always — if you think it's wrong, say so in a note and keep your
version. That's what the button is there for.

## 10. Report a problem
- Every audio part has a **"Report a problem"** button. Use it to describe anything wrong —
  **you can write in Japanese**.
- It automatically saves the exact text and audio you were looking at, so we can see precisely
  what you saw and fix it.
- Follow your reports (and read replies) under **"Bug reports"** in the top bar — a red badge
  appears when there's a reply for you.

## Don't add words just to fix the sound
If a word comes out mispronounced, it's tempting to change the **kana** until it sounds right by
adding an extra word. Don't — the text on screen is what the learner reads, so anything you add
ends up in the finished trip. Use **"Fix pronunciation…"** instead (see section 6): you type a
clearer reading, it re-records the audio from that, and **the text on screen stays exactly as it
was**. (Changing the *reading* in the kana line is fine — that IS the pronunciation. It's adding
extra *words* that causes trouble.)

## Changed your mind about a part?
**"Revert to original"** (bottom right of each part) puts that part **completely back to how it
started** — the text, the English, and the original recording. Use it if an edit turned out to be
a mistake and you want to start again from the original.

## Handy to know
- The **? button in the top bar** opens this guide and a one-page quick reference at any time.
- **Everything saves automatically** as you type.
- **Undo / Redo** step backwards and forwards through audio versions if you change your mind.
- You can leave a **comment** on any part for David.
- If you get logged out, just log in again — your work is saved.
