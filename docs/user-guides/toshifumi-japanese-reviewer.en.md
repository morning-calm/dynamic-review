# Review App — Guide for Toshifumi (Japanese trips)

*Plain-English how-to. You don't need to know anything technical — this is only how to
review and correct the Japanese text and audio for each trip.*

## What this tool does
It replaces the old "Google Doc + audio editor" process. You open a trip, correct the
Japanese text, fix the audio, listen, mark each part done, and submit. David then approves it.

## 1. Log in
1. Open the link David sends you.
2. Enter your **username** and **password**. If it doesn't work, message David — don't share
   the link publicly.

## 2. Find your trips
- The home page shows the trips waiting for you (only Japanese trips are shown to you).
- Any trip with a **pin** at the top is urgent — do those first.
- Click a trip to open it.

## 3. Review each part, from top to bottom
A trip has, in order: the **trip Title**, the **trip Description**, then the **Scenes**. Each
scene has a **Title**, the **Narration** (called *SceneDesc*), and sometimes a **Question**
with **Options**.

Read each part against the English and **correct any mistakes**.

## 4. Important: which line is spoken
Each Japanese narration has **two lines**:
- **Kanji line** (top) — the written form.
- **Kana line** (bottom) — the reading.

**The voice speaks the KANA line.** So:
- To change **the audio**, you must edit the **kana** line.
- Editing only the kanji line changes the written text but **not** the audio. The app shows a
  small reminder under the narration to make this clear.

## 5. Fix the audio after you change the words
1. Correct the **kana** line.
2. Click **"Generate from edit"** — the app re-records **just the part you changed** and keeps
   the rest of the original recording.
3. **Listen** to the new version.
4. If it's good, click **"Combine"** to keep it.
5. If it's not right:
   - **"Regenerate whole block"** re-records the entire line, or
   - **"Create new"** lets you attach a fresh take **with a note** for David.

## 6. Mark each part "Done"
- **Play the audio all the way to the end** (the progress bar must fill). Only then does the
  **Done** button unlock. Click it.
- Text-only parts (with no audio) can be marked Done straight away.

## 7. Submit the trip
- When **every** part is marked Done, click **Submit**. David reviews and approves it.
- After you submit, the trip becomes read-only until David approves or sends it back.

## 8. Report a problem *(new button — being added)*
- Next to each part there will be a **"Report a problem"** button. Use it to describe anything
  wrong — **you can write in Japanese**.
- It automatically saves the exact text and audio you were looking at, so we can see precisely
  what you saw and fix it. You'll get a reply in the app when it's fixed.
- Until that button is live, use the **comment box** on the part and set the flag to
  **"Edit required"** to hand it to David.

## Handy to know
- **Everything saves automatically** as you type.
- **Undo / Redo** step backwards and forwards through audio versions if you change your mind.
- You can leave a **comment** on any part for David.
- If you get logged out, just log in again — your work is saved.
